ml 基础概念

Hessian矩阵：设有凸函数f(X)，X是向量（x1,x2,…, xn)，Hessian矩阵M定义为：M的第i行,第j列元素为df(X)/dxidxj, 即为f(X)对于变量xi和xj的二次偏导数。
梯度：设有凸函数f(X)，X是向量（x1,x2,…, xn)，函数f(X)在点X0处的梯度是一个向量，等于（df(X0)/dx1, df(X0)/dx2, …., df(X0)/dxn), 即是对于各个变量的偏导数的向量。例子：如果方程是z=f(x,y)，梯度是在XOY平面内的一个向量，与z无关。因此要特别注意梯度不是点(X，f(X))处的切线方向。

牛顿法

$$
\theta^{t+1}= \theta^{t}-\frac{l(\theta^{t})}{l’(\theta^{t})}
$$
牛顿迭代法是一个收敛速度非常快的方法，被称为二次收敛(quadratic conversions), 牛顿方法的每一次迭代都会是你的解的有效数字提高一倍。即error:0.1->0.01->0.0001->0.00000001,当然，这是在解足够逼近真实解的情况下。
若$\theta$是向量的话，为：
$$
\theta^{t+1}= \theta^{t}-H^{-1}\Delta_{\theta}l \\
H_{ij}=\frac{\partial^2l}{\partial\theta_i\partial\theta_i}
$$
where H is the Hessian Matrix.
$$
P(y|x,\theta) \\
y \in R, Guassian \rightarrow least squasres \\
\mathcal{N}(\mu, \delta) \\
y \in {0,1},Bernoulli \rightarrow Logistic \quad regression \\
Bernoulli(\phi) \rightarrow P(y=1;\phi)=\phi \\
$$

exponential family

$$P(y;\eta)=b(y)e^{\eta^TT(y)-a(\eta)}$$
$\eta$- natural parameta 自然分布
$\tau$ - sufficient statistic 充分统计量
T(y)=y
$\eta$ 是一个实数
给定的概率分布即：a,b,t
我们改变不同的a,b,t的取值，得到不同的分布

$$
Ber(\phi) \\
P(y;\phi) = \phi^y(1-\phi)^{1-y} \\
= e^{log \phi^y(1-\phi)^{1-y}} \\
= e^{ylog\phi+(1-y)log(1-\phi)} \\
= e^{log\frac{\phi}{1-\phi}y+log(1-\phi)}
$$
where:
$$
1 \rightarrow b(y) \\
log\frac{\phi}{1-\phi} \rightarrow \eta \\
y \rightarrow T(y) \\
-log(1-\phi) \rightarrow a(\phi)
$$
对于Guassian 分布：
$$
\mathcal{N}(\mu,\sigma^2) \quad set \sigma = 1 \\
P(y)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}}e^{-\frac{1}{2}(y-\mu)^2} \\
=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}}e^{-\frac{1}{2}y^2}e^{\mu y-\frac{1}{2}mu^2} \\
where \\
\frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}}e^{-\frac{1}{2}y^2} \rightarrow b(y) \\
\mu \rightarrow \eta \\
y \rightarrow T(y) \\
-\frac{1}{2}mu^2 \rightarrow a(y)
$$
也同样可以分拆成指数族的形式。

The Mahalanobis distance of an observation $ {\vec {x}}=(x_{1},x_{2},x_{3},\dots ,x_{N})^{T}$ from a set of observations with mean $\vec {\mu }=(\mu_{1},\mu _{2},\mu _{3},\dots ,\mu _{N})^{T}$ and covariance matrix $S$ is defined as:

$$ D_{M}({\vec {x}})=\sqrt {({\vec {x}}-{\vec {\mu }})^{T}S^{-1}({\vec {x}}-{\vec {\mu }})}$$
Mahalanobis distance (or “generalized squared interpoint distance” for its squared value[3]) can also be defined as a dissimilarity measure between two random vectors $ \underline {x} $and$\underline {y}$ of the same distribution with the covariance matrix $S$:
$$ d({\vec {x}},{\vec {y}})=\sqrt{ ({\vec {x}}-{\vec {y}})^{T}S^{-1}({\vec {x}}-{\vec {y}}) }$$
If the covariance matrix is the identity matrix, the Mahalanobis distance reduces to the Euclidean distance. If the covariance matrix is diagonal, then the resulting distance measure is called a normalized Euclidean distance:
$$
d(\vec{x},\vec{y})=\sqrt{\sum_{i=1}^N {(x_i - y_i)^2 \over s_{i}^2}},
$$
where $si$ is the standard deviation of the xi and yi over the sample set.
因此，两个样本在两个不同的分布中的马氏距离是不一样的，除非这两个分布的协方差刚好一样。

Convolution And Deconvolution

Convolution

The convolution of f and g is written f∗g, using an asterisk or star. It is defined as the integral of the product of the two functions after one is reversed and shifted. As such, it is a particular kind of integral transform:
$$(f*g)(t)=\int_{-\infty}^\infty(\tau)g(t-\tau)d\tau \\
=\int_{-\infty}^\infty(t-\tau)g(\tau)d\tau$$
蓝色方块为输入，filter是$3\times 3$的卷积核，PADING=2，stride=1;上面藏青色方块为卷积结果。

Deconvolution

In mathematics, deconvolution is an algorithm-based process used to reverse the effects of convolution on recorded data.
